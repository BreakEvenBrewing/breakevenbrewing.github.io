    Bottling Beer

    Our process for bottle conditioning beer

    Equipment Needed

    1. Beer Bottles and Caps(~12 per gallon)
    2. Bottle Caper
    3. Bottle Brush
    4. Bottling Bucket
    5. Tubing and Bottling Wand
    6. Bottle drying tower and sanitizing hand pump
    7. Star-San
    8. Priming sugar
    9. Transfer tubing from fermenter

    Hazards Identification and PPE / Safety Steps Required:

    PPE: Rubber boots

    Hazardous Products: Star-San

    Hazards: N/A

    Preparation

    1. Taste the beer to be bottled. Confirm that Dialectal is not present.
    2. Rinse, Bottle Brush, and Rinse every bottle. Inspect to see that all detritus is gone. In the case where the bottle will not come clean, set aside to soak in PBW later.
    3. Mix 1/4oz Star-San with 1/2L warm tap water in sanitizer hand pump. Give every bottle two pumps to coat the inside with sanitizing solution, then invert on rack to dry.
    4. Clean and Sanitize bottling bucket, tubing, and bottling wand. Make sure to run some Star-San solution through the spigot/tubing/bottling wand.
    5. Soak the bottle caps in a Star-San solution to sanitize
    6. Prepare priming sugar solution. Use this calculator to determine the quantity of sugar to add for the style you are bottling. Heat to a boil to sanitize.
    7. Sanitize fermenter transfer tubing

    Steps

    1. Add hot priming sugar solution to the bottom of the bottling bucket.
    2. Transfer beer from fermenter to bottling bucket.
    3. With bottling wand, depress into bottom of bottle and fill to 1mm below the lip of the bottle. Removing the wand from the bottle will create the appropriate headspace.
    4. Cap the bottle with the bottle caper and place into case
    5. Clean and rinse all items used in bottling that came in contact with the beer.
    6. Store at 68-74f for 7 days to allow the yeast to carbonate the beer, cellar.
